# Ring 0031 — the "Seed" name is cleared as a non-exclusive descriptive codename: the trademark knockout search landed, no registration is claimed, and a public commercial launch is the revisit trigger

- Date: 2026-07-17
- Stage: 3 — Flowering
- Raised-by: gardener
- Question: [E-004](../plans/entropy-ledger.md), the last Stage 3 → 4 gating prerequisite (plan
  [0005](../plans/active/0005-flowering.md)): "Seed" / "The Seed" is an uncleared working codename
  (SEED.md §8; ring [0004](0004-name-hosting-visibility.md) settled *what it is called*, not its legal
  clearance). SEED.md §8 requires "clear trademark/naming before any external use," and the entry's
  interest rate spikes to high at Stage 3 because pollen cannot ship *externally* under an uncleared
  name. The ledger set the price exactly: **a trademark search plus Gardener judgment.** With the search
  done, the judgment: what does "cleared" mean for a self-hosting, open-source (MIT), non-commercial
  methodology whose base case (SEED.md §7) is one Gardener with many *private* repositories?
- Decision: **Retain "The Seed" as a non-exclusive, descriptive codename — the seed claims no trademark
  and seeks no registration.** The name is cleared *for use* in the seed's current and base-case posture
  (open-source, non-commercial, self-host-first, and pollination of the Gardener's own private repos);
  it is **not** cleared as an exclusive/protectable mark, because such a mark is neither available nor
  needed here. The honest clearance is "usable, not owned." A public *commercial* launch under the bare
  name is the revisit trigger (below), to be taken with professional counsel.
  - **The knockout search — the LAW-6 evidence.** A preliminary search over the US register (via public
    mirrors) and the AI / developer-tools landscape on 2026-07-17; it is *not* a legal-clearance opinion
    (the seed carries no lawyer, ring [0024](0024-intake-network-free-metabolizer.md)) and it is
    primarily US-focused (a full multi-jurisdiction EUIPO/WIPO clearance was not run). It found "Seed" /
    "The Seed" to be one of the most crowded, weak names in and around AI/dev software:
    - *Senior users in the exact space.* **ByteDance "Seed"** — its foundation-model division and family:
      the Seed LLM, the open-source `seed-oss` LLM, Seedream, Seedance (litigated — the Paramount/Disney
      cease-and-desist over Seedance 2.0, Feb 2026). Notably ByteDance coins *distinct* marks for actual
      frameworks (e.g. DeerFlow, a multi-agent framework, TM filed May 2025) rather than shipping "Seed"
      as a product name. **seed.run — "SEED"** — a YC W21 managed CI/CD pipeline for serverless (the SST
      team): a developer-tools SaaS branded literally SEED. **SeedsAI**, **Seed AI** (Montreal, 2018),
      **Seed Innovations** (IonQ acquisition, Jan 2026) — AI-software companies named "Seed(s) AI".
    - *Registered bare wordmark + phonetic twin.* **SEED** — Seed Health, Inc., US Reg. 5629609, a bare
      wordmark (Class 5, probiotics): proof the bare word is registered and defended, in another field.
      **Seeed Studio** (since 2008) — open-source edge-AI hardware serving developers, phonetically
      identical.
    - *Generic usage.* "seed" is a generic engineering term (database seeding, RNG seed, seed-round
      funding); no prominent AI *agent framework* is literally named "the-seed".
  - **The assessment.** A bare "Seed" / "The Seed" wordmark for AI/dev software (Nice Class 9/42) is
    realistically **not registrable** — a §2(d) likelihood-of-confusion refusal or an opposition is
    likely given the senior in-space users, and the term is suggestive-verging-descriptive and generic in
    dev usage; there is little to acquire as exclusive rights. **Current risk is low**: nothing is sold
    under the mark, the use is metaphorical/descriptive (a self-propagating "seed"), and no pollen has
    shipped externally. Risk becomes real only at a public *commercial* launch — so registration is
    neither available nor warranted now.
- Alternatives considered:
  - **Split the outward brand** — keep "The Seed" internally, choose a distinct, clearable name for
    external/public pollen. Deferred, not rejected: it front-loads a naming task and a second name to
    maintain before any public launch exists. Folded into the revisit-when, so it is chosen (with
    counsel) *if and when* a public commercial offering is real, not speculatively now.
  - **Rename now to a distinctive/coined mark** (the ByteDance "DeerFlow" strategy). Rejected for now:
    highest cost — it ripples through the SEED.md genome, the public `the-seed` repository, the README,
    and every doc — and it is premature with no commercial intent and a private-repos base case. The
    revisit-when reopens it if that changes.
  - **Register "SEED" as a trademark now.** Rejected: it is not available (the crowded senior-user field
    above) and not needed (non-commercial, MIT). Pursuing it would spend Gardener judgment and filing
    money for a probable refusal.
- Enforcement: doc-only, justified. Naming/legal clearance is a meta-level judgment with no in-repo
  artifact to lint — the exact posture ring [0004](0004-name-hosting-visibility.md) established for the
  name ("meta-level choices with no in-repo artifact to lint yet; the ledger prices the naming debt").
  The observable proof is this recorded decision plus the revisit-when triggers; consistent with the
  decision, the seed makes **no** ™/® claim anywhere and asserts no exclusive rights. The debt moves to
  the entropy ledger's **Paid** section (E-004), closing the LAW-8 entry, and the Stage 3 → 4 transition
  is no longer gated on a name.
- Revisit-when: the seed (or a descendant) is taken to a **public, commercial** launch under the name
  "The Seed" / "Seed"; **or** a conflict surfaces (a cease-and-desist, an opposition, or a demand from a
  senior user such as ByteDance Seed, seed.run, or Seed Health); **or** the Gardener decides to seek
  registration or a distinct outward brand — at which point professional counsel is engaged and the
  split-brand / rename alternatives above reopen; **or** the name is changed for any other reason.
